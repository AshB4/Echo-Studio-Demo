# Facebook Style Guide

Purpose: collect examples and notes for future Taste Skill voice passes on Facebook posts.

## Examples to Collect

- Posts that feel personal without oversharing.
- Product posts that do not read as hard-sell ads.
- Short updates that invite comments or reactions.
- Behind-the-scenes posts connected to a real project or decision.
- Successful weekend or low-frequency posts for cautious account lanes.
- Posts with media where the caption and image work together.

For each example, capture:

- Date.
- Campaign or product.
- Account or page.
- Full post text.
- Media used.
- Result, if known.
- Why it should influence future drafts.

## Writing Characteristics to Preserve

- Direct, human wording.
- Specific lived context instead of generic advice.
- Light humor only when it sounds natural.
- Practical observations.
- Clear product relevance when selling.
- Enough warmth to fit Facebook without becoming vague.

## Formatting Preferences

- Prefer short paragraphs.
- Avoid dense blocks of text.
- Use line breaks when they improve scanning.
- Keep CTAs simple and conversational.
- Include affiliate disclosure when required.
- Avoid hashtag stuffing.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

- Facebook can carry more personal context than Pinterest or DEV.
- Do not cross-post technical article copy without softening the entry point.
- Product links need clear context before the link.
- Treat personal-profile lanes as cautious and lower-frequency.
- Keep Facebook variants distinct from LinkedIn by reducing business jargon and increasing personal specificity.

## Avoid Notes

- Avoid generic "check this out" captions.
- Avoid repeated emotional hooks.
- Avoid performative vulnerability.
- Avoid reposting the same product angle too frequently.
- Avoid text that sounds like an ad template.
