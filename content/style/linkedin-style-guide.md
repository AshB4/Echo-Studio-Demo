# LinkedIn Style Guide

Purpose: collect examples and notes for future Taste Skill voice passes on LinkedIn posts.

## Examples to Collect

- Posts that connect product, operations, or publishing work to a business lesson.
- Founder/operator reflections with specific details.
- Short tactical posts that teach something without sounding like a thread template.
- Launch, update, or build-in-public posts.
- Posts that use a restrained CTA.
- Strong comments or replies that show the voice in conversation.

For each example, capture:

- Date.
- Topic.
- Audience.
- Full post text.
- Result, if known.
- Why it worked.
- Any edit that made it stronger.

## Writing Characteristics to Preserve

- Clear point of view.
- Practical insight from experience.
- Business-aware language without jargon.
- Specific examples instead of abstract lessons.
- Confidence without inflated claims.
- Measured pacing.

## Formatting Preferences

- Lead with the concrete situation or claim.
- Use short paragraphs for scanability.
- Use bullets only when the idea benefits from a list.
- Keep CTAs low-pressure.
- Avoid excessive hashtags.
- Avoid fake thought-leader cadence.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

- LinkedIn can support more strategic framing than Facebook.
- Preserve business relevance, but do not turn every post into a lesson.
- Keep product mentions tied to customer or operator value.
- Prefer one clear insight over several loosely connected points.
- Rework DEV-style technical detail into outcome, decision, or tradeoff language.

## Avoid Notes

- Avoid "here is what I learned" formulas.
- Avoid generic leadership phrasing.
- Avoid inflated transformation claims.
- Avoid three-part list posts that all sound the same.
- Avoid performative contrarian hooks.
