# Pinterest Style Guide

Purpose: collect examples and notes for future Taste Skill voice passes on Pinterest pin copy and visual packaging.

## Examples to Collect

- Pins with strong save intent.
- Product pins that make the use case obvious.
- Seasonal pins that match real search behavior.
- Pin titles with clear audience, outcome, or occasion.
- Descriptions that support discovery without keyword stuffing.
- Image/caption combinations that performed well.

For each example, capture:

- Date.
- Product or campaign.
- Board.
- Pin title.
- Pin description.
- Image used.
- Keywords or seasonal context.
- Result, if known.
- Why it should guide future drafts.

## Writing Characteristics to Preserve

- Clear visual promise.
- Specific use case.
- Search-friendly wording.
- Practical benefit.
- Simple product context.
- Save-oriented framing.

## Formatting Preferences

- Keep titles readable and specific.
- Use natural keyword phrases.
- Put the strongest visual or use-case phrase early.
- Avoid overloaded descriptions.
- Keep affiliate disclosure and link behavior aligned with platform rules.
- Pair alt text with what the image actually shows.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

- Pinterest copy should support discovery, not conversation.
- Every pin needs a clear reason someone would save it.
- Seasonal content needs enough lead time.
- Avoid repeating the same hook across a batch.
- Distinguish product, idea, and tutorial pins.

## Avoid Notes

- Avoid vague aesthetic captions.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Avoid repeated pin titles with only tiny changes.
- Avoid hard-sell phrasing.
- Avoid publishing images that do not match the promise of the title.
